Different types of galaxies

How a galaxy is formed

Galaxies are mainly composed of dust, stars and gas. No one knows exactly how many galaxies exist. Galaxies themselves can be split into three types: irregular galaxies, spiral galaxies and elliptical galaxies.

Irregular Galaxies

This type of galaxy appears misshapen and generally lacks form. This is due to the fact they are influenced by the gravitational pull of other galaxies close by.

Spiral Galaxies

This includes our own galaxy, the Milky Way. This type has a bulging centre with spiral arms and a flat disk. This disk includes stars, dust, gas and planets which all rotate around a specific galactic centre.
