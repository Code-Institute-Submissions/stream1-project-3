In this section you'll get an overview of the different planets

  • There are now a total of 8 planets. Previously there had been 9, but scientists have now claimed Pluto to be a moon. These 8 planets are as follows: Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus and Neptune.

  • Mercury is the closest planet to the Sun. It also doesn’t have any moons. One year on Mercury would be equivalent to 88 days on Earth. Heavy metals and rock are mainly what make up the composition of this planet, making it very dense when compared to its size.

  • Venus is the second planet from the sun, with one year being the same as 225 days on Earth. It also has a similar size as Earth.

  • Even though Earth has a greater amount of mass and volume, Earth and Mars have similar levels of landmass. This is due to the fact that two thirds of Earth is covered in water. There is also 37% less gravity on Mars when compared to Earth.

  • According to Nasa, Jupiter is the largest planet in our solar system. It's that big 1,300 Earth's could actually fit inside!

  • Saturn is the sixth planet in the solar system, and is a gas planet. One day on Saturn consists of 10.7 Earth hours.

  • Uranus turns on its axis in the opposite direction to that of Earth and most other planets. It takes 84 years for it to orbit the Sun once. In this time it has 42 years of sunlight with the other amount of time being fully set in darkness.

  • Neptune is the eighth planet from the sun, and is the smallest ice giant planet. Hydrogen, helium and methane are the main elements that are present in Neptune’s atmosphere. It also has 14 moons, one of which is called Triton. This moon is surrounded by nitrogen ice and dust.
