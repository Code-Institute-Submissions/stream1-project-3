Now it's time to learn about the stars

The way stars are formed

The Sun is the closest star to Earth. Stars are formed inside gas and dust, which is also known as molecular clouds. They are extremely cold which allows the gases to cause the atoms to bind together. The life cycle of a star depends on its size. There are also approximately 200 billion stars in the universe, but the exact number isn't known.

Red Giant Star

After a star has consumed its hydrogen fuel, there is now no longer an outward pressure to offset the inward pressure holding it together. Once it completely runs out of fuel and this process has reached the end of its cycle, the star will become a white dwarf star

White Dwarf Star

A white dwarf star is formed when a star has fully run out of hydrogen fuel. This causes the star to collapse inward from its own gravity. A white dwarf star will now start cooling down, which actually takes billions of years

Red Dwarf Star

This is the most common type of star. This type of star is able to keep its hydrogen fuel, allowing them to keep burning for much longer when compared to other stars

Supergiant Star

These types of stars are the largest in the universe, often referred to as monsters due to their massive size. They also consume hydrogen at a much faster rate, and will therefore use their core fuel much quicker. This also means they will die much younger

Supernova Star

This beautiful burst of light is the aftermath of the death of a star. Amazingly, a supernova has the potential to briefly release enormous energy levels, often rates that are even greater than what the sun emits
